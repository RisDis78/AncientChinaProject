Evidence:

The Ancient Chinese government wanted to protect themselves
from northern invaders while trading so they built the Great Wall of China.
The Great Wall of China costed more than a 1 million lives.

Reasoning:

Geography is an important part of culture. It is where you live. There is a good side and bad side of geography.
The good side of geography is the Great wall of China. They provided trade to the northern “Huns” without being attacked.
This accelerated trade because they didn’t have to follow safety precautions.
The wall protected them. Now the Great Wall of China is a landmark which leads to culture.
So shining this in a positive light show trade does affect culture.
Now leading to the bad side of geography is when Emperor Qin murdered and buried workers for taking a rest.
This scared off people as it could be dangerous.
This gave a bad tarnishing name to the Great wall of China and ultimately affected ancient Chinese culture in a bad light.
This is how geography shows that trade can affect culture.
